Copyright Policy

Introduction

The Pan Pacific University Library is committed to respecting intellectual property rights while supporting academic research and learning. This policy outlines the guidelines for fair use of copyrighted materials within our library system.

All library users must comply with copyright laws and fair use guidelines when using library resources.

Fair Use Guidelines

Permitted Uses

  • Single copies for personal study
  • Limited portions for academic research
  • Brief quotations in academic work
  • Classroom use of excerpts

Restrictions

  • Mass copying or distribution
  • Commercial use of materials
  • Creating course packets without permission
  • Systematic copying of articles

Digital Resources

Electronic Resources

  • Access limited to authorized users
  • Downloads for personal academic use only
  • No mass downloading or systematic copying
  • Respect license agreements

Database Usage

  • Individual research purposes only
  • No sharing of login credentials
  • Comply with vendor terms of use

Compliance and Violations

Reporting Violations

Report suspected copyright violations to the Library Administration Office. All reports will be treated confidentially and investigated promptly.

Consequences

  • Warning for first-time violations
  • Suspension of library privileges
  • Referral to university disciplinary committee
  • Possible legal action for serious violations

Repeated or willful copyright violations may result in permanent loss of library privileges and/or legal action.
